Mali and the Magic Guitar Picks

Hey Mali :]

Right now I’m sitting in the yard on a lovely Spring afternoon, and I hope you’re having a nice day too. It’s been a while! I’ve been thinking of you, and I’ve wanted to tell you this story for a while. It happened almost a year ago, after the last time we had a beautiful jam, and I’d like to call it “Mali and Magic Guitar Picks” - sounds like a nice song or folk tale.

The morning before our jam I’d been in a music store dealing with obnoxious guitar dudes, which I’m sure you’re familiar with. At checkout I decided to pocket some beautiful guitar picks (partly out of spite). There was one particular lovely tortoise-shell gold one that stuck out to me, and I brought it with me to the jam with you. When I shared with you that song that I’d been writing, I used that lovely little gold pick to play your guitar. Using that pick and playing that song with you felt a bit magic. I’d been feeling a bit of stuck with it, but jamming on it with you it felt different, it felt loud, and it helped me gt unstuck for a time.

I didn’t mean to, but I left the gold guitar pick tucked under the strings of your guitar when I left, or maybe I dropped it in your big reverby room. I realised I’d lost it while I was on the three-hour train trip back home. I was like “oh no my magical guitar pick” for a minute, but then I thought that maybe you would find it and use it and fill it up with even more magic and one day I’d see you using it.

The beautiful part is that when I got off at the platform I looked down and between my feet was an almost identical guitar pick, in a gorgeous green to match the gold. It was a bit of spooky action at a distance!

I thought I’d tell you this story next time I saw you, and hopefully I’ll see you soon, we’re just such busy people. I can’t believe it’s been almost a year - that time has flown by! That little bit of magic from you sustained me for a while, but I’ve still had a difficult relationship with that one song. Just recently I found that magic green pick again in my pocket, and used it to revisit that song. I’m trying to seeing it through this time, as an act of self-love, because sometimes you need to put things to rest. Anyways, hope you have a beautiful birthday and I’ll see you soon! Can’t wait ’til we next get to jam.

Big love,
Olive ⁂

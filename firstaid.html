the patient is a person first.

Encourage the casualty to treat themselves, for example, apply pressure to their own bleeding wound.

Do not move the casualty, unless: - there are any immediate threats to life such as water, fire and explosion - you are unable to provide life saving measures in their current position, such as when you need to provide CPR and the casualty is on a bed - the patient is unconscious and breathing, so should be moved into the recovery (side-lying) position.

Do not touch a vehicle or attempt to rescue a person within 10m of a fallen power line.

You should only remove a helmet to assist with breathing, maintain an open airway, manage bleeding or provide CPR. Motorbike helmets provide support to the head, neck and spine and can help prevent further injury.

Managing the casualty’s airway and any severe bleeding takes priority over cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) and all other treatments. This takes priority over other injuries (including any possible head, neck and spinal injuries).

  • First, check for any airway obstruction by carefully and gently turning the casualty onto their side. Their mouth should be open and tilted slightly downwards to allow any fluid to drain.
  • Remove any solid material using a sweeping motion with the fingers.

For a casualty who has drowned, roll them onto their side to allow water to drain and clear the airway.

If the casualty vomits during CPR, roll the casualty onto their side, and manually clear their airway, before rolling them onto their back and continuing CPR.
